# KPI Documentation

## Project Title
Carbon Emission Impact Analysis & Sustainability Intelligence Platform

---

## 1. Introduction
This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) used in the Carbon Emission Impact Analysis project. These KPIs help measure environmental sustainability, energy efficiency, and economic impact across countries.

The KPIs support business analysis, ESG monitoring, sustainability benchmarking, and decision-making.

---

# 2. KPI Definitions

---

## KPI 1: Average CO₂ Emissions Per Capita

### Definition
The average amount of carbon dioxide emissions generated per person within a country.

### Formula
CO₂ emissions per capita

### Dataset Source
Carbon Emissions Dataset

### Business Purpose
Used to identify countries with high environmental impact and compare carbon emission intensity across regions.

### Business Interpretation
- Higher values indicate greater pollution levels
- Lower values indicate better environmental performance

---

## KPI 2: Average Renewable Energy Percentage

### Definition
The average percentage of total energy consumption derived from renewable energy sources.

### Formula
Renewable Energy %

### Dataset Source
Renewable Energy Dataset

### Business Purpose
Measures sustainability adoption and clean energy transition progress.

### Business Interpretation
- Higher values indicate stronger renewable adoption
- Lower values suggest fossil fuel dependency

---

## KPI 3: Average GDP

### Definition
Average economic output of a country over the analysis period.

### Formula
Average GDP

### Dataset Source
World Bank GDP Dataset

### Business Purpose
Used to compare economic growth with environmental impact.

### Business Interpretation
- High GDP may correlate with industrial activity
- Helps assess economic-emission relationships

---

## KPI 4: Average Energy Consumption

### Definition
Average per capita energy consumption.

### Formula
Average Energy Consumption

### Dataset Source
Energy Consumption Dataset

### Business Purpose
Measures energy demand intensity.

### Business Interpretation
- Higher values indicate greater energy demand
- May contribute to increased emissions

---

## KPI 5: Carbon Intensity

### Definition
Measures environmental impact relative to economic output.

### Formula
Carbon Intensity = CO₂ Emissions / GDP

### Dataset Source
Derived KPI

### Business Purpose
Evaluates how efficiently economies generate output with lower emissions.

### Business Interpretation
- Lower values indicate cleaner economic productivity
- Higher values indicate carbon-heavy economies

---

## KPI 6: Energy Efficiency

### Definition
Measures economic productivity per unit of energy consumed.

### Formula
Energy Efficiency = GDP / Energy Consumption

### Dataset Source
Derived KPI

### Business Purpose
Evaluates efficient energy utilization.

### Business Interpretation
- Higher values indicate better energy efficiency
- Lower values indicate inefficient energy usage

---

## KPI 7: Sustainability Score

### Definition
Composite metric used to rank countries based on sustainability performance.

### Example Logic
Higher renewable energy
+
Lower CO₂ emissions
+
Better energy efficiency

### Dataset Source
Derived KPI

### Business Purpose
Benchmark country sustainability performance.

### Business Interpretation
- Higher score indicates stronger sustainability leadership
- Lower score indicates environmental risk

---

## KPI 8: Emission Growth Trend

### Definition
Measures changes in emissions over time.

### Formula
Year-over-year CO₂ change

### Dataset Source
Carbon Emissions Dataset

### Business Purpose
Track environmental progress or deterioration.

### Business Interpretation
- Rising trend indicates worsening emissions
- Falling trend suggests sustainability improvement
